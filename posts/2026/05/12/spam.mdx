## Spam is totally fine

Let's try to explore what the spam is, how it does work and how it can help us.

When I check my email inbox, the most messages is created by robots. Most of them are irrelevant. Most of the irrelevant messages such bad that my email service automatically moves them into junk folder. Some messages alive the spam filters but when I open them I see the call to action requests, but don't see any insightful information so I click the button and use these letters to improve my spam filter.

Sometimes I see a brilliant messages that has been sent via automation. I noticed they are never seen like "Do you want to get known about it? Contact me if you are interesting!", they rather include all the information, they are insightful and I learn something new when I read such messages.

The sender intention to sell me something does not matter for me when I get known something worthful. Sometimes I really interesting in their service and I click links, but I mostly don't.

The social media tries to marginalize an irrelevant messages they have not been paid for. As result they hide the uncut gems from us.

## The fight against spam

I have an account in X, and every time I tries to open the feed page, I see totally irrelevant content - a random screenshots with code and "beautiful" interfaces, the "insights" about AI, the "interesting" facts, the influencer trash talks. It's just because Twitter thinks I'm developer so AI garbage may be interesting to me.

However I don't see in my feed the content from a random persons with a short and meaningful blog posts.

That's because the Twitter algorithms show commercial spam above others. People will see your posts in their feed only if you pay for promotion, pro plan, etc.

I've tried to find some ways to read the independent writers, and I've tried to publish my own writings. I mostly ended up with a deletions of my links and bans.

For example the Reddit as platform sometimes automatically deletes posts you sent. It deletes your posts with no reason and randomly. You can ask moderator of "subreddit" where you posted to recover your post and sometimes they do. But reddit did not asked their opinion and ban your post automatically prematurely.

That does not mean Reddit have no spam. There are many well known strategies how to promote anything on Reddit, for example people creates many accounts, fake its activity, creates a thread that looks like a questions, and then send their spam from another accounts. Reddit pretends it looks legit, because well faked users activity brings money to reddit.

As you may notice, the problem of status quo is that **only non-commercial spam can be blocked**. Any spam that is efficiently makes more money than spends will not gone.

The fight against spam is mostly theater. The platforms get rid independent non-commercial publishers, end users only see commercial spam, like influencer posts that pretend to be insightful, but actually sell you yet another service they paid for.

The platform makes more money and are happy, users waste their lives to see the ads on the screen.

## Do not remove a spam

> Well ok, we currently see mostly commercial spam that does not feels useful.<br/>What we can do to see better content?

My answer is don't block a spam.

Moreover, if you want to find worthful content you should look for more spam.

The less moderation and auto filters platform have, the greater chance to find something interesting.

Look for a marginal platforms where spam is not blocked well yet. For example, instead of Twitter try for Bluesky or Mastodon, they are flooded with a spam, so you can just filter it by interesting tags, ban few accounts that appears frequently with garbage, and sometimes you will find interesting content.

Don't lock yourself in a Facebook or a dating apps. Be wild, be out of mainstream to find interesting things ahead of others.
